    About My Internship

    Where

    Paediatric Association of Jamaica

    Supervisor

    Dr Bovette Butler

    Responsibilities

    Strengthen social media platforms for NGO
    Provide technical support

    The internship was done through the Heart Foundation which incited a programme which would help NGOs to grow and develop their social media pages. It was a remote internship where I was placed at the Paediatric Association of Jamaica. While I was aware of the basic components such as creating posts for the pages and researching topics for each post, I had never managed the analytics of a business page before. The opportunity allowed me to grasp the ins and outs of social media management while employing various media monitoring aspects. While I had hoped that my internship would have been one that would allow me to work with other like-minded persons in my field and learn under a team, I was more so alone having to figure things out. This was a great learning opportunity to explore my creativity, research, and show initiative.

    Reflection

    The internship provided me with the opportunity to be creative in managing their social media platform. While I do very well working in team-dependent situations, I believe that some amount of autonomy can be valuable for personal and professional growth and lead to me expanding my ideas for content. Creating a marketing strategy for the company is a significant achievement as I had never had to do that outside of the bounds of school assignments. It challenged my overall ability to think strategically and contribute to the organization's overall goals. I also grew a much better understanding of how analytics are a crucial part of social media management. It helped me understand which posts would perform better as well as how they would do depending on when they were posted. I had to factor in the days for posting as well as the time when I worked around my schedule for posts.

    Final Thoughts

    I was generally underwhelmed with the late start to the internship as certain information such as the login information was inaccessible to me for a few weeks resulting in a delay in posting and limiting the amount of posts I would have liked to do for the page. Seeing that the internship was put on by the Heart Foundation which placed me with the Paediatric Association, I found that the information relayed between the two seemed to be very vague. When meeting with persons from the PAJ for the first time, they seemed somewhat confused with my role in managing their social which I went forward to explain. For this reason, I would not want to work with this particular company again but I still valued the experience as a good learning opportunity. I would have also wanted something that would have challenged me more and learned more than managing a social media account. Having a remote internship with flexibility provides advantages in terms of work style and global collaboration. In my future job searches, I intend to actively seek out opportunities that align with my professional development goals and offer a level of challenge that I find stimulating and rewarding.
